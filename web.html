Imagination Library of Grant County

How it Began

The Imagination Library of Grant County is a New Mexico chapter of Dolly Parton’s Imagination Library. After visiting the current site and showing a few colleageus, me and my colleagues took it upon ourselves to redesign their site, as Dolly Parton and her non-profit work is close to our hearts. Our aim was to refurbish the site in way that looked modern, but also functioned and felt modern as well.

Research

Before the design process, we decided the most important part of our redesign was understanding the primary issues were; after discussion decided that one key factor was how quickly the user understood the interface, so we set up a 5 second usability test to see what users noticed first and foremost.

Next we set up a usability interview to see where users would navigate to in real time while also observing and taking note of struggles, confusion, and feelings that interveiwees encountered while the browsed the old site.

We gathered that a key issue from the original site was it’s visual orginization, so we decided the best move was to minimalize the UI and reconsolidate the site’s content so the user had to click less to get farther. Early on we decided that a card based layout was the best way to convey the info we felt was getting burried or missed.

As we began to iterate on our lo-fi frames we began looking at inspiration from some of the more modern design and color trends, and went with a look we felt was kid friendly and also engaging enough that parents wouldn’t get tried of visiting. We then went into iterating some of the iconography.

Final Product

Our final redesign ended up being a great marriage of creative freedom and practical design, with usability and manueverability at the forefront of the experience.

A clickable Figma link is here, so you can view our final creation!

After we presented our final product to the imagination Library of New Mexico branch, they adopted the designs, and are currently in the proccess of integrating our updates!
