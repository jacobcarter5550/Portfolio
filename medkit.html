Medkit

OVERVIEW

A Health Platform Designed to Manage News, Vaccine/Testing Appointments, and Events.

Having lived with Cocid-19 for almost a full year now, my team and I came together and thought about some of the biggest issues we all felt were affecting us the most, on the day to day. Just amongst ourselves we felt that news was hard to keep up with, maintaining social health was just as challenging, and keeping track of where we could get which treatments was also confusing. We put our heads together to come up with Medkit.

    • PROBLEM

    The general public struggles to keep Covid-19 mentally well managed.

    As information regarding COVID-19 circulates, people are unsure of who and what to believe. Additionally, frustrations with testing and the vaccine roll out are at an all time high. As the year anniversary of the lockdowns quickly approaches, people feel as though their social health has withered without the ability to meet with those they’d normally see regularly.

    DESIGN CHALLENGE

    How might we help an individual keep up to date on Covid-19 in a comfortable, non-overwhelming way?

    SOLUTION

    Medkit: A platform to access Covid-19 news, schedule tests and vaccines, and access online events to stay social.

    RESEARCH

    How did we get here?

    We knew that amongst ourselves Covid had certainly taken its toll, but to make sure we weren’t the only ones we put together a survey to get info on exactly how other people were feeling with the pandemic being almost a year old.

    Our questions in the survey mostly centered around how participants felt about some common topics like the fear towards contracting Covid or how they’d fair after contraction, how they stay up to date on Covid news, how many people they personally knew that had the virus, and general frustrations towards the topic in general (we got some very political responses to that question). Some of our key takeaways are below.

    Insights

    • A majority of respondents, 81%, have not caught COVID

    • 25% have not been tested

    • 27% have been tested 5+ times

    • Over 50% of respondents know 1-5 people who have contracted COVID-19

    Pain Points

    • Wide spread misinformation

    • The pandemic has become politicized

    • Lack of info/clear direction with the vaccine roll out

    • Not sure who to listen to, and what info to pay attention to

    IDEATION

    Defining the Problem

    How might we create a platform with consistent access to reliable information and tools, so users and loved ones can stay safe and healthy?

    As we came off the back of our initial research, we decided we need to be a tad more precise with our expectations and goals; ‘fix life during lockdown’ seemed a bit too broad and impractical, so we narrowed things down. We came to three main issues that we felt consistently came up during research, and could practically be addressed.

    We decided to break our platform up into three parts:

    Health Spot

    • Locate testing/vaccine centers close to you

    • Sign up for tests/vaccines

    • Access test results

    • Check your symptoms

    Info Hub

    • Easy access to important information at a glance

    • Access real time news from vetted & reliable sources

    • Share articles through app, sms, email, or social media

    Community

    • Find COVID approved activities

    • Join support groups

    • Access self care and mental health resources

    • Share events with others

    Coming away from this phase of the project felt good; we had a clearer picture of what it was that we needed to prioritize, how to sparse apart our content, and what we felt was within our reach of accomplishment, and what wasn’t.

    PROTOTYPING

    Starting to Build

    With the conceptual structure of our platform put together, we began to softly frame out some sections of the app. We started by tackling what we thought the first couple hurdles that we felt really needed to get right were: the way that news was presented to users and the reminders that users had when they were in the app.

    We tried not to spend too much time reinventing the wheel, however when we initially started working on what would eventually be the Info Hub, we experimented with a few rudimentary layouts for what we thought a good delivery of info would look like.

    When it came to reminders and events within the app, our philosophy was centered around avoiding the user having to search for their scheduled event, whether it’s a test, or a social meeting.

    We ultimately ended up having the event-reminder tab be subjective to which tab users were on; if a user were in Health Spot, they’d see a reminder for their testing appointment, but not the virtual event they signed up for.

    Styling and More Styling

    After having thought out loud through our wireframes, we turned to some more mid-fidelity thinking, and began to lay out some of our styling preferences. Our design philosophy was in an unusual predicament; we wanted to convey the trust and reassurance of a medical app, but that’s not what Medkit was. We ended up combining some tropes from medical apps, white backgrounds, solid colors, and filled buttons, with glass-morphism. We felt that the aesthetic that glassmorphism had worked well with the pseudo-medical effect that we were pining for

    Iterations

    Once our visual direction had been set in place, we began to iterate on some of our lofi mockups, applying our current stylistic choices onto them. Initially throughout our first phases, we leaned too heavily on glass-morphism as a basis, but near the end, there was a more equal distribution on glass-on-solid stylings.

    During one of our first passes, we had an entirely glass-based interface, with a duality between our primary red and blue.

    After assessing that some of the previous designs left too much space, we began filling our pages in.

    Nearer to some of our more final iterations, we circled back to the desire to include our primary red consistently into our interface, however we felt that it’s presence would be more of an indication of danger or urgency, and dialed back its presence.

    Next Steps and Reflections

    Stepping away from our final product to take a look was exciting. We were all really happy with how things turned out, and while we felt we’d successfully created something that satisfied the vision we shared, we of course saw ways that our process could’ve improved. We felt like some areas of our development, iterating on lofi mockups to mid-fi, for example, could have been a bit more expeditious. We also felt that some potentially important features were missing from our final product; we listed some of our further additions and iterations below.

    • Build out a fully realized feature to access test results

    • Expand platform’s capabilities to be applicable outside of Covid-19, potentially expanding to focus on mental health, new strains of Covid, or other viruses

    • Build a feature that tracks Covid statistics based on state and county, eventually international tracking

    • Flesh out ‘registration’ sign up flow in the Community section

    This project was one of our favorites to work on, and was a product of some of our best collaboration. I hope you enjoyed the journey.
